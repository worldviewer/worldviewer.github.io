  • Paper at arXiv.org. Verschuur has a couple of fantastic books on these subjects. See his book, The Invisible Universe, where he emphasizes how astronomers initially considered radio waves from space either a hoax or a mistake. His book, Interstellar Matters, contains a couple of chapters which in great detail explain how to read a radio astronomy paper. With not much more than that, Verschuur's numerous IEEE papers will become accessible to most laypeople. His papers on interstellar HI hydrogen and critical ionization velocities (CIV's) are particularly noteworthy because they appear to suggest that ionization is an ongoing process in space -- directly contradicting what is taught to astrophysicists today. Verschuur frequently questions the use of the term "cloud" to describe the interstellar matter structures, due to its unusually filamentary nature. Although his publications do not directly raise the question of electricity in space (due to ongoing resistance to the idea amongst peer reviewers), it is clear to many that he is entertaining the notion.
